GEOGRAPHY

Welcome to a comprehensive exploration of Western Highlands Province (WHP) and its distinctive districts. Western Highlands Province is not only defined by its awe-inspiring landscapes, rich culture, and historical significance but also by the unique characteristics of each district that contribute to its vibrancy. Let's delve into what makes our province and its districts special.

I. Overview: WHP

1.1. Geographic Location

Western Highlands Province is centrally situated within Papua New Guinea, a nation renowned for its cultural diversity and breathtaking geography. Bordered by Enga Province, Southern Highlands Province, Hela Province, and Jiwaka Province, WHP encompasses an expansive and diverse terrain.

1.2. Provincial Capital

The provincial capital, Mount Hagen, serves as both an administrative center and a gateway to the province's treasures. This urban hub lies nestled amidst fertile lands and is a vibrant melting pot of cultures.

1.3. Landmass and Topography

Covering a significant landmass of approximately 10,157 square kilometers, Western Highlands boasts a diverse topography. From rugged mountain ranges, including the famous Mount Wilhelm, to fertile valleys and highland plateaus, the province's terrain is as varied as its culture.

1.4. Population and Communities

WHP is home to a diverse population of Papua New Guineans, each belonging to various ethnic groups. These communities are known for their unique traditions and languages, contributing to the cultural mosaic of Papua New Guinea.

1.5. Agriculture and Cash Crops

Agriculture plays a pivotal role in the province's economy. The fertile soil of Western Highlands is conducive to the cultivation of cash crops like coffee, tea, and vegetables. These crops not only support local livelihoods but also contribute significantly to the nation's export revenues.

1.6. Key Industries and Businesses

The province's key industries include agriculture, mining, manufacturing, and services. Numerous businesses, ranging from small local enterprises to larger corporations, are engaged in these sectors. The province's economy is diversified, ensuring resilience and growth.

1.7. Source of Income

The primary source of income for many residents of Western Highlands is agriculture, with coffee being a major cash crop. Additionally, income is generated from trade, services, and the extraction of mineral resources.
